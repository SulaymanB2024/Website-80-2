Not all housing markets are created equal. National forecasts mask critical bifurcation: some metros will see 3-5% price growth in 2026, while others face 5-10% declines. To understand this divergence, we applied k-means clusteringAn unsupervised machine learning algorithm that partitions data into k groups by minimizing within-cluster variance across features. to 92 major metros using six fundamental variables: (1) inventory-per-sale ratio, (2) YoY home price change, (3) rent growth rate, (4) income-to-buy ratio, (5) new construction permits per capita, and (6) job growth rate. The algorithm identified four distinct market regimes that explain 78% of the variance in Zillow's 2026 price forecasts.

"Markets cluster not by geography, but by dynamics. Austin and Boise are twins; Boston and Buffalo are strangers."

Cluster 1: Structural Squeeze (Forecast: +2.5% to +3.8%)

Characteristics: Low inventory-per-sale (<1.5), strong job growth (>2.5%), limited new construction, stable rent growth (3-5%). These markets never overbuilt and continue to attract net in-migration. Supply constraints are structural, not cyclical.

Representative Metros: San Diego, Seattle, Boston, Portland (OR), San Jose

Thesis: Supply-inelastic marketsMarkets where regulatory constraints, geographic limitations, or land scarcity prevent supply from responding to demand shocks. with durable demand. Geographic constraints (water, mountains) and zoning restrictions limit new supply. Tech and knowledge-economy job bases provide income support. Price appreciation will be modest but persistent-the "least bad" markets in a challenging macro environment.

Cluster 2: Supply Catch-Up (Forecast: -2.1% to +0.8%)

Characteristics: Elevated inventory-per-sale (2.5-3.5), strong 2021-23 home price run-up (+40-60%), high new construction permits (5-8 per 1,000 residents), decelerating rent growth (<2%).

Representative Metros: Austin, Phoenix, Boise, Tampa, Nashville

Thesis: The pandemic boomtowns. These markets saw explosive demand in 2020-22, triggering a construction wave that is now flooding supply just as mortgage rate sensitivity crimps demand. Inventory-per-sale ratios have doubled or tripled. The forward path is mean reversion-prices must correct to align with local income fundamentals. Austin's 2026 forecast: -2.1%. Phoenix: -1.3%. These are the "supply overshoot" markets.

Cluster 3: Stabilization Grind (Forecast: +0.5% to +1.8%)

Characteristics: Balanced inventory-per-sale (1.8-2.2), moderate price growth 2021-24 (+25-35%), steady job and income growth, normalized rent trajectory (2-4%).

Representative Metros: Denver, Atlanta, Charlotte, Dallas, Minneapolis

Thesis: These are the "Goldilocks" markets-not too hot, not too cold. They saw price appreciation during the boom but didn't overheat. Construction kept pace with demand, preventing severe undersupply. Now they're settling into a low-growth equilibrium. Expect 1-2% annual price gains-essentially flat after inflation. Stability, not excitement. For investors, these are cash-flow markets, not appreciation plays.

Cluster 4: Legacy Decline (Forecast: -1.5% to -0.2%)

Characteristics: Elevated inventory (>3.0 months), negative or flat job growth, aging demographics, weak rent growth (<1%), legacy industrial economic base.

Representative Metros: Detroit, Cleveland, Pittsburgh, St. Louis, Buffalo

Thesis: Secularly declining marketsMarkets experiencing long-term structural headwinds from out-migration, aging populations, and economic base erosion.. These metros face structural out-migration as younger workers move to sunbelt and tech hubs. Housing supply exceeds demand, but prices are already near replacement cost, limiting downside. Forecast: modest nominal declines or stagnation. Real prices (inflation-adjusted) will fall 3-5%. These are value traps-avoid unless yield compensates for depreciation risk.

Key Insights from the Clustering Analysis

1. Geography is noise, dynamics are signal: Austin (TX) and Boise (ID) cluster together despite being 1,200 miles apart. Both experienced pandemic-driven oversupply. Boston (MA) and San Diego (CA) cluster together due to structural supply constraints.

2. Price momentum is mean-reverting: Markets with the strongest 2021-23 gains (Cluster 2) face the weakest 2026 outlooks. Markets with modest gains (Cluster 3) show steady forward trajectories. The lesson: avoid chasing momentum.

3. Job growth matters more than price history: The clustering algorithm weighted job growth 2.3x more heavily than recent price changes in explaining 2026 forecasts. Employment drives household formation, which drives housing demand. Price history is backward-looking; job growth is forward-looking.

Portfolio Allocation Strategy for 2026

• Overweight Cluster 1 (Structural Squeeze): Defensive positioning. Buy for wealth preservation and modest appreciation. Best risk-adjusted returns in a downturn scenario.
• Underweight Cluster 2 (Supply Catch-Up): Avoid new purchases. If already exposed, consider tactical exits in H1 2026 before tax-loss selling season. Opportunistic buyers should wait for further declines (target: -8 to -12% from peak before entry).
